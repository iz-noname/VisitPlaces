OUTDOOR ACTIVITIES

Come out and play

Taste the outdoors

There is no reason to stay indoors. Here are some fun things to do, whether you are visiting the coast, the mountains or somewhere in between. Please remember to stay safe.

From mountain biking down rugged alpine terrain to navigating an aerial assault course across a forest canopy, to scaling the lofty heights to gliding long the shoreline of an island in sea kayak. From world-class watersports to walking, cycling to aqualining, the possibilites for getting active in magnificent great outdoors are endless.

Find your new favourite activity!

Hiking

Take the walk on the wild side

Rays of sunlight are finally showering the landscape, forcing the snow in the mountains to a silent retreat, and waking the flowers after their winter sleep. Enter the start of the hiking season. After a long winter, people of all age groups and social backgrounds head outdoors and go trekking in the mountains, along the coast or in the forests. And you are more than welcome to join us in our unashamed love affair with nature.

Cycling

Rides through magnificent nature

High mountains, steep downhills and rough terrain – at first sight, many countries may seem like a cycling destination reserved for hard-core road cycling, mountain bike and downhill enthusiasts. And sure, if you want a challenge on two wheels, we can help you with that!

Snowsports

The secret to skiing is in the details

At the high mountain ski resorts the season lasts from autumn through spring, and – if based at a glacier – some are even open through the summer. While there are cross­country trails and alpine resorts all over the country, the largest and most popular destinations are located in the mountains. In addition to lifts, slopes and trails, these will offer accommodation, good foods and nightlife.

Canoeing & Kayaking

Cruise ships are fun, but if you really want to get up close and personal with the still and deep bay waters or the wilder coastline, kayaks or canoes are more intimate options.

The narrow fjords, majestic mountains and a variety of islands provide a really special setting. Here you can find your very own beach, stay in a traditional fishermen’s cottage and experience the local culinary traditions as part of your paddling tour.
